Ausbildung in Germany

What is an Ausbildung?

Definition: An Ausbildung is Germany's vocational training system. It combines paid practical work in a company with classes at a vocational school. After 2 to 3.5 years you earn a recognized professional qualification.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Clear, honest answers about Ausbildung in Germany.

What is Ausbildung in Germany?

Ausbildung is Germany's vocational training system. It combines paid practical work in a company with classes at a vocational school. After 2 to 3.5 years you earn a recognized professional qualification.

Can foreigners do Ausbildung in Germany?

Yes. International applicants can do an Ausbildung in Germany. The exact requirements depend on the profession, the company, your language level, your country of origin and your personal situation.

Do I need German language skills?

Usually yes. Many companies and vocational schools ask for German at around B1 to B2 level, because training and daily work mostly happen in German. Some technical and IT roles can be more flexible.

Do I get paid during Ausbildung?

Yes, in most cases. Trainees receive a monthly training salary that usually increases each year. The exact amount depends on the profession, region and company.

How long does Ausbildung take?

Most Ausbildung programs take 2 to 3.5 years, depending on the profession. Strong performance can sometimes shorten the duration.

Can I stay in Germany after Ausbildung?

Often yes. Many people who complete an Ausbildung continue working in Germany, and there are residence options for qualified workers. The exact rules depend on your situation and current German law.

What documents do I need?

Typically a valid passport, your school certificates (often with certified translations), a CV, language certificates, and the company training contract. The exact list depends on the profession and your country.

Do I need a visa?

If you live outside the EU, you usually need a national visa for vocational training before you arrive. Requirements depend on your nationality and personal situation, so always check with the German embassy in your country.

Is Ausbildung the same as university?

No. University is mostly academic study with little or no pay. Ausbildung is practical, paid training inside a real company combined with vocational school, leading to a job-ready qualification.

Which Ausbildung jobs are popular in Germany?

In-demand fields include healthcare and nursing, hospitality, IT, logistics, retail, technical and industrial professions, skilled trades, and office and business roles. Demand varies by region and year.
